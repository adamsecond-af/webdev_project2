Travel and Architecture

Why I Like to Travel

Traveling helps me reset and gives me a new perspective. I like exploring places that feel different from home, trying new food, and learning how people live day to day. Even short trips feel meaningful when I come back with new memories and ideas.

I also like travel because it pushes me to be present. Whether it is walking through a new neighborhood or finding a viewpoint at the end of a long day, it makes me slow down and actually notice details.

Architecture is What I Notice First

Architecture is one of the main reasons I get excited to visit new places. I like seeing how buildings are designed, and history of the area. Sometimes the most interesting parts are the small details like textures, courtyards, and patterns.

I enjoy comparing older structures to modern buildings and noticing how materials and shapes change over time. I also like places where you can walk around and see a mix of styles in just one area.

What I Look For On a Trip
  • Walkable areas where I can explore without rushing.
  • Buildings with history, unique materials, or interesting shapes.
  • Neighborhoods that show everyday life and local culture.
  • Viewpoints, parks, and public spaces that connect people.
  • Museums or tours that explain the background of the architecture.
